I've always done things my own way.

As a boy my parents would find me playing with the cables behind the TV instead of watching the TV.

When I was finishing school I started a blog where I did strange things like eating breakfast in a tree or filling my entire room floor with autumn leaves for a photo.

After school I toured Europe on foot, hitchhiking without money and taking the boat to Iceland where I lived for two months. I later biked from Sweden to Norway on a city bike I found on the side of the road, without a phone or a map to help guide me.

I've relied on the kindness of strangers throughout my life because I believe in the power of an idea.

What idea, you ask?

Well, it's the idea that people are good and that most of us simply want to connect and share experiences.
If you have a bit of common sense and a moderate ability to read peoples intentions, life isn't so dangerous.

None of us have to play by the rules. Not really.

Try something new, life is best served with a side of fun.
